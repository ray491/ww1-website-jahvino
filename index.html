Introduction

Great Britain entered World War 1 on 4 August 1914 after Germany's invasion of Belgium. As the heart of the British Empire, Britain mobilized millions, fielding the British Expeditionary Force (BEF) and dominating the seas with the Royal Navy. The war cost over 900,000 British lives and reshaped the nation.

Key Battles

Battle of the Somme (1916)

Bloodiest day in British history: 57,470 casualties on July 1. Total: 420,000 British losses.

Battle of Jutland (1916)

Royal Navy vs German High Seas Fleet. Britain maintains naval supremacy despite losses.

Passchendaele (1917)

Muddy hell: 260,000 British casualties for minimal gains.

Key Leaders

David Lloyd George

Prime Minister (1916-22). War leader who pushed for victory.

Douglas Haig

BEF Commander. Controversial for Somme/Passchendaele tactics.

John Jellicoe

Grand Fleet commander at Jutland.

Royal Navy & Home Front

The blockade starved Germany. Home Front: Rationing, women in factories (munitionettes), Zeppelin raids.

Legacy

Victory but at huge cost. Empire weakened, led to independence movements. Remembrance: Poppy, Unknown Soldier.
